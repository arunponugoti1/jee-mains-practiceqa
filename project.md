**Physics — Ray Optics (JEE Mains)**

Ray optics treats light as straight-line rays that reflect and refract at surfaces. The two workhorses are the **mirror formula** (1/v + 1/u = 1/f) and **Snell's law** (n₁sinθ₁ = n₂sinθ₂). Critical angle and total internal reflection happen when light tries to go from denser to rarer medium beyond a threshold angle. Lenses use the same mirror formula logic but with the **lens maker's equation** for construction. JEE Mains heavily tests sign convention errors, combination of lenses/mirrors, and prism deviation — these are the most common traps.

**Chemistry — Group 17 (Halogens)**

Halogens are the most reactive non-metals — one electron short of a noble gas configuration, so they're aggressive oxidising agents. Reactivity decreases F > Cl > Br > I as you go down (bond dissociation energy and electron affinity drop). Fluorine is the odd one out — no positive oxidation states, no oxyacids except HOF, and it oxidises water. Key JEE areas: interhalogen compounds (XX' type), oxoacids of chlorine (HOCl < HClO₂ < HClO₃ < HClO₄ in acid strength), and the bleaching action of Cl₂. Trends in bond length, colour, and state (F₂ pale yellow gas → I₂ violet solid) are direct one-liners in Mains.
